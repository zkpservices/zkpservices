# dApp Guide

<div className="mt-8"></div>
Using zkp.services is simple. All available services are accessible through the [dashboard](/dashboard) once you have 
set up your account. Onboarding to new chains simply involves selecting a new chain from a dropdown, however the other
services are slightly more involved and are covered in more detail in this guide. {{ className: 'lead' }}

<div className="lead">

<div className="font-bold">Some core services:</div>
- <div className="font-semibold">[Requesting Data](/guides#request-data)</div> Securely request a data snapshot and leverage custom MFA if needed.
- <div className="font-semibold">[Requesting Updates](/guides#request-update)</div> Securely request a data update from another user or a smart device and leverage custom MFA if needed.
- <div className="font-semibold">[Cross-Chain Backups](/guides#cross-chain-backups)</div> Back up your data with Web2/Web3 providers, including CCIP or EVM-compatible chains.
</div>

## <div className="mb-4 font-bold">Request Data</div>

Before you get started, here are all the values you will need to Request Data:

<table className="border-collapse border border-gray-200 w-full">
  <tr>
    <th className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2 text-left">VALUE</th>
    <th className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2 text-left">DESCRIPTION</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Request</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">This is the data field (e.g. "Medical Records") you want to request.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Key</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">A secure key to help decrypt data once you receive it.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Time Limit</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">Time in seconds before the recipient must respond to your data request.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>2FA Information (optional)</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">If you want added security, you can make it so that the responder has to undergo a 2FA requirement before responding.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Response fee</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">A data request will cost 10 ZKP tokens by default. If you require more ZKP tokens from whom you request data, increase the number.</td>
  </tr>
</table>


### <div className="font-bold">STEP 1: Initiate a data request</div>

Once you are ready to initiate a data request, navigate to the dashboard. Here you will find the "Request Data" card. Click it to open a modal that will allow you to request data.

### <div className="font-bold">STEP 2: Fill out your data request</div>

You will be presented with the form below. Below are brief descriptions covering each input you must complete before requesting data.

<table className="border-collapse border border-gray-200 w-full">
  <tr>
    <th className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2 text-left">INPUT</th>
    <th className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2 text-left">DESCRIPTION</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Address of Receiver</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">The wallet address containing the requested data.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>One Time Key</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">Once you have received the requested data, the one-time key will be used to access it. You can put in your own or generate a random one. Either way, make sure you save it somewhere.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Time Limit of Request</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">The time limit dictates how long your request will be active for the requestee to respond in seconds.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Require 2FA</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">If selected, the requestee must complete 2 Factor Authentication ("2FA") to respond to your request. An additional set of inputs will be included if you decide to attach 2FA to your request.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2"><b>Set Response Fee</b></td>
    <td className="border border-gray-300 px-4 py-2">The response fee will be the number of ZKP tokens the requestee requires to answer your request.</td>
  </tr>
</table>

Once you have completed the form, clicking “Call Smart Contract” will initiate the data request.

### <div className="font-bold">STEP 3: Confirming your data request was sent</div>

Once you have initiated the data request, you will see that request in your recent activity at the bottom of the dashboard. Navigate to the "Outgoing" tab, and you can check the status of your data request here.

Once you have initiated the request to update data, you will see that request in your recent activity at the bottom of the dashboard.

Navigate to the "Outgoing" tab, and you can check the status of your data request here.

After the recipient approves your request, you can use your key and salt to verify the data has been changed.
